# Voting Rules

Voting should be fair, simple, and separate from the main judging result.

## Audience Voting

- Audience members can vote for Team A, Team B, or Abstain
- Audience voting should open after both teams have presented
- Audience voting should decide People's Choice only
- Audience voting should not decide the main champion

## Judge Scoring

Use a 100-point rubric:

| Category | Points |
|---|---|
| Usefulness and real-world impact | 25 |
| Technical execution and reliability | 20 |
| UI/UX and accessibility | 15 |
| Teamwork, leadership, and role balance | 15 |
| Open source quality | 15 |
| Presentation clarity and demo quality | 10 |

## Awards

- Best Project: highest total judge score
- Best Leadership: strongest leadership score with judge notes
- Best Technical Execution: strongest technical score with judge notes
- Best UI/UX: strongest design score with judge notes
- Best Community Contribution: strongest teamwork and contribution score with judge notes
- People's Choice: audience vote only

## Tie-Breakers

If two teams are tied, break the tie in this order:

1. Higher usefulness score
2. Higher open source quality score
3. Higher technical execution score
4. Judge chair decision with a short written note

## Fairness Rules

- Keep the vote secret until the end
- Use the same judging sheet for both teams
- Do not let popularity decide the main award
- Keep the event friendly and respectful
