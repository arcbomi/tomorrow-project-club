# Code of Conduct

The club should be safe, respectful, and welcoming.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind and patient
- Help beginners learn
- Give credit for real work
- Share space and time fairly
- Keep disagreements focused on the project, not the person
- Respect privacy and consent

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment or bullying
- Gatekeeping or mocking beginners
- Cheating in votes or demos
- Stealing work or claiming other people's contributions
- Sharing private student or school information
- Blocking others from participating because they are new

## Team Culture

- Competition should stay friendly
- Leaders should support the team, not control it
- Maintainers should guide work, not dominate it
- Everyone should be able to ask questions without being embarrassed

## Reporting Problems

If something feels unsafe or unfair, tell the club maintainer or the school advisor as soon as possible.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to club meetings, repository activity, messages, presentations, and public events.
